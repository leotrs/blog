My interpretation of "Some observations on the smallest adjacency eigenvalue of a graph", by Cioaba et al.

Rewrite of Theorem 2.1

It is understood that \(V_j \subset V \) and \(w_j(uv) = 0\) if either \(u\) or \(v\) is not in \(H^j\).

This result concerns a weighted graph \( G (V, w) \) and a collection of graphs \( D = \{ H^j (V_j, w_j) \}_{j=1}^{m} \) such that \[ w(uv) = \sum_j w_j (uv) \] for each edge \(uv\) in \(G\). In this case, \(D\) is called a decomposition of \( G \).

\( \lambda(H) := \min_i \{ \lambda_i: \lambda_i \text{ is an eigenvalue of } H \} \) \( D_u := \{ H^j \in D: u \in H^j \} \) \( \lambda(D_u) := \sum_{H \in D_u} \lambda(H)\)

The main goal is to show how to use such a decomposition to find lower bounds for the smallest adjacency eigenvalue of \(G\). In the following, let \( \lambda(H) \) be the smallest adjacency eigenvalue of a graph \(H\). Further, let \(D_u\) be the set of graphs in \(D\) that contain \(u\), and define \( \lambda(D_u) \) as the sum of \( \lambda(H) \) across all \(H\) that contain \(u\).

Theorem (2.1 in [??]) Let \(D = \{ H^j \}\) be a decomposition of \(G\). Then, \[ \lambda(G) \geq \min_u \lambda(D_u). \]
