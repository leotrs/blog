About

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences, working in Jürgen Jost's Complex Systems group. My research advances the mathematical foundations of network science through spectral graph theory and its applications to machine learning.

I earned my PhD in Network Science from Northeastern University under Tina Eliassi-Rad, where I developed novel spectral methods for graph analysis. My dissertation introduced new theoretical frameworks for understanding non-backtracking operators on networks.

My work has been published in leading venues including Physical Review E, IEEE Transactions on Network Science, and Journal of Complex Networks. I have presented at international conferences including NetSci, SIAM Applied Linear Algebra, and Complex Networks.

Research Program

Spectral Theory of Non-Backtracking Operators

My primary research program develops the spectral theory of non-backtracking operators on graphs. Unlike traditional graph matrices (adjacency, Laplacian), the non-backtracking matrix is non-normal, requiring novel analytical techniques from functional analysis and operator theory.

Key contributions include: (i) Complete characterization of the spectrum for regular graphs, (ii) Perturbation theory for eigenvalues under graph modifications, (iii) Applications to network immunization and epidemic control.

Key Publications:
  • L. Torres, K.S. Chan, H. Tong, T. Eliassi-Rad. "Nonbacktracking Eigenvalues under Node Removal: X-Centrality and Targeted Immunization" (2021) [arXiv]
  • L. Torres, P. Suárez-Serrato, T. Eliassi-Rad. "Non-backtracking Cycles: Length Spectrum Theory and Graph Mining Applications" Applied Network Science (2019) [journal]

Geometric Methods in Network Analysis

I develop geometric approaches to network analysis that leverage differential geometry and algebraic topology. This research program connects the local geometry of graph embeddings to global network properties.

Recent work includes the GLEE (Geometric Laplacian Eigenmap Embedding) algorithm, which preserves geometric structure in dimensionality reduction, achieving state-of-the-art performance on link prediction tasks.

Key Publications:
  • L. Torres, K.S. Chan, T. Eliassi-Rad. "GLEE: Geometric Laplacian Eigenmap Embedding" Journal of Complex Networks (2020) [journal]
  • L. Torres, A. Sizemore Blevins, D. Bassett, T. Eliassi-Rad. "The why, how, and when of representations for complex systems" SIAM Review (2021) [journal]

Mathematical Epidemiology on Networks

During the COVID-19 pandemic, I applied network science methods to understand disease spread on mobility networks. This work combined rigorous mathematical modeling with large-scale data analysis of human movement patterns.

Our findings informed public health policy by quantifying the impact of mobility restrictions on disease transmission in major US metropolitan areas.

Key Publications:
  • B. Klein et al. (including L. Torres). "Reshaping a nation: Mobility, commuting, and contact patterns during the COVID-19 outbreak" Nature Human Behaviour (2020) [journal]
